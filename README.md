# Obsidian Vault - PARA Method

Personal knowledge management system organized using the PARA method by Tiago Forte.

## What is PARA?

PARA is a universal system for organizing digital information, created by Tiago Forte. It stands for:

- **P**rojects
- **A**reas
- **R**esources
- **A**rchives

This system is designed to be simple, actionable, and focused on what you're working on right now.

## The Four Categories

### 📋 Projects (01-projects/)

**Definition**: A series of tasks linked to a goal, with a deadline.

**Characteristics**:
- Has a specific end goal
- Has a clear deadline or timeframe
- Once completed, it moves to Archives
- Examples: "Launch new website", "Plan vacation to Japan", "Complete course on Python"

**Questions to ask**:
- What am I working on right now?
- What are my current commitments?
- What needs to be finished by a specific date?

### 🎯 Areas (02-areas/)

**Definition**: A sphere of activity with a standard to be maintained over time.

**Characteristics**:
- No end date
- Ongoing responsibility
- Requires continuous attention
- Examples: "Health & Fitness", "Finances", "Professional Development", "Home Management"

**Questions to ask**:
- What areas of my life require ongoing attention?
- What standards do I want to maintain?
- What roles and responsibilities do I have?

### 📚 Resources (03-resources/)

**Definition**: A topic or theme of ongoing interest.

**Characteristics**:
- Reference material
- Learning resources
- Topics you're interested in
- May be useful in the future
- Examples: "Web Development", "Photography Tips", "Book Notes", "Recipes"

**Questions to ask**:
- What am I interested in learning about?
- What topics do I want to explore?
- What information might be useful someday?

### 📦 Archives (04-archives/)

**Definition**: Inactive items from the other three categories.

**Characteristics**:
- Projects that are completed or no longer active
- Areas you're no longer responsible for
- Resources that are no longer relevant
- Keeps your active folders clean and focused
- Can be referenced if needed

**Questions to ask**:
- What projects have I completed?
- What responsibilities have ended?
- What can I set aside for now?

## The PARA Workflow

### 1. Capture Everything in Inbox (00-inbox/)
All new information starts here. This is your temporary holding area for unprocessed notes, ideas, and information.

### 2. Process and Organize
Regularly review your inbox and ask:
- Is this **actionable** with a **deadline**? → Project
- Is this an **ongoing responsibility**? → Area
- Is this **reference material** or **learning content**? → Resource
- Is this **no longer active**? → Archive

### 3. Review Regularly
- **Daily**: Check active projects and move tasks forward
- **Weekly**: Review all four folders, process inbox, move completed projects to archives
- **Monthly**: Ensure areas are being maintained, clean up resources

## Why PARA Works

1. **Action-Oriented**: Organized around what you're doing, not theoretical categories
2. **Simple**: Only four top-level folders
3. **Flexible**: Works across all tools and platforms
4. **Scalable**: Grows with your needs without becoming overwhelming
5. **Focused**: Keeps you focused on active work by separating inactive items

## How to Use This Vault

1. **Start with Inbox**: Capture everything in `00-inbox/` without worrying about organization
2. **Process Weekly**: Move notes from inbox to appropriate PARA folders
3. **Work from Projects**: Focus most of your attention on `01-projects/`
4. **Maintain Areas**: Regularly check `02-areas/` to ensure standards are met
5. **Reference Resources**: Use `03-resources/` as your personal knowledge base
6. **Archive Completed Work**: Move finished projects and outdated items to `04-archives/`

## Key Principles

- **Projects vs Areas**: If it has a deadline or end goal, it's a Project. If it's ongoing, it's an Area.
- **Less is More**: Keep your system as simple as possible
- **Actionability**: Organize by actionability, not by topic
- **Mobility**: Information should flow between categories as its status changes
- **Clean Workspace**: Archive aggressively to keep your active workspace focused
