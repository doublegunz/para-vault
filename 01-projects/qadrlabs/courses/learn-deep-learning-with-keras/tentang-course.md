# Learn Deep Learning with Keras: From Neural Networks to CNNs, RNNs, and Transformers

## Deskripsi
Step into deep learning with Keras in Google Colab. Build neural networks from scratch, train them well, then create CNNs for images, RNNs for sequences, and meet Transformers, ending with an end-to-end image classifier.

## Konten
You finished the machine learning series. You can build and evaluate models, prepare data, and tune pipelines with scikit-learn. Now it is time to move from classical machine learning into deep learning, where the model learns its own features instead of relying on the ones you hand it.

This course picks up right where "Machine Learning: Beyond the Basics" left off. You still work entirely in Google Colab, but you switch from scikit-learn to Keras, the high-level deep learning library that ships with TensorFlow. Keras feels familiar because its `fit` and `predict` calls mirror scikit-learn, so you can focus on the new ideas instead of new syntax. You start with what a neural network really is and how it learns, then make your networks train well by choosing good activations, architectures, and regularization. From there you teach networks to see with convolutional neural networks, reuse powerful pretrained models with transfer learning, handle sequences like text with recurrent networks and LSTMs, and finally build an intuition for the attention mechanism behind modern Transformers.

The course favors intuition and hands-on practice over heavy math. Each module builds on the last, and you finish with a capstone where you assemble a complete, saved image classifier from start to finish. By the end you will be comfortable building deep learning models and ready to go deeper on your own.

**This course is for you if:**
- You completed the machine learning courses or know classical ML (the workflow, training, evaluation, and data prep)
- You can train a scikit-learn model but want to understand and build neural networks
- You keep hearing terms like neural network, CNN, LSTM, embeddings, and Transformer, and want to actually use them
- You are ready to move from classical machine learning into deep learning

**Prerequisites:**
- Completed "[Machine Learning: Beyond the Basics](https://qadrlabs.com/course/learn-machine-learning-beyond-the-basics)" or equivalent knowledge
- Comfortable with: the train/test workflow, `fit`/`predict`, basic metrics, and overfitting
- Basic Python with NumPy and pandas
- A Google account to use Google Colab (free)

**By the end, you will have:**
- A solid mental model of how neural networks learn, from loss and gradients to optimizers
- The ability to build, train, and evaluate networks with Keras
- Practical skills to fight overfitting with dropout, regularization, and early stopping
- Hands-on experience with CNNs for image classification and transfer learning
- Experience with RNNs and LSTMs and text classification using embeddings
- An intuition for attention and Transformers through a pretrained model
- A complete, saved, end-to-end image classifier
- A clear map of where to go next in deep learning

## Daftar Modul

### 1. Module 1 - Deep Learning Foundations
Understand what deep learning is, how it differs from classical machine learning, and set up Keras with a free GPU in Google Colab so every example runs fast in your browser.

- Lesson 1 - What Is Deep Learning?
- Lesson 2 - Setting Up Keras in Google Colab

### 2. Module 2 - Neural Network Fundamentals
Build your first neural network with Keras and learn how it actually learns, from loss functions and gradient descent to optimizers, epochs, and batches.

- Lesson 3 - Your First Neural Network with Keras
- Lesson 4 - How Neural Networks Learn: Loss, Gradients, and Optimizers

### 3. Module 3 - Training Deep Networks Well
Make your networks train better with the right activations and architecture, then stop them from overfitting using dropout, regularization, and early stopping.

- Lesson 5 - Activations, Layers, and Network Design
- Lesson 6 - Fighting Overfitting: Dropout, Regularization, and Early Stopping

### 4. Module 4 - Computer Vision with CNNs
Teach networks to see with convolutional layers, build an image classifier from scratch, and reuse powerful pretrained models with transfer learning.

- Lesson 7 - Convolutional Neural Networks
- Lesson 8 - Building an Image Classifier
- Lesson 9 - Transfer Learning with Pretrained Models

### 5. Module 5 - Sequence Models
Handle ordered data like text and time series with recurrent networks and LSTMs, then build a text classifier using word embeddings.

- Lesson 10 - Recurrent Neural Networks and LSTMs
- Lesson 11 - Text Classification with Embeddings

### 6. Module 6 - Attention and Transformers
Discover the attention mechanism behind modern AI and see how Transformers work by using a pretrained model for a real task.

- Lesson 12 - The Attention Mechanism
- Lesson 13 - Transformers in Action with a Pretrained Model

### 7. Module 7 - Capstone and Next Steps
Bring everything together in a complete, end-to-end image classifier, then map out where to go next in your deep learning journey.

- Lesson 14 - Capstone: An End-to-End Image Classifier
- Lesson 15 - What's Next
